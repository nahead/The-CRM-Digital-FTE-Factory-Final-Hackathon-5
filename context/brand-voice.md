# TechCorp Brand Voice & Communication Guidelines

## Brand Personality

**TechCorp is:** Helpful, Professional, Approachable, Efficient, Empathetic

**TechCorp is NOT:** Robotic, Overly Casual, Condescending, Pushy, Corporate-Speak

## Core Principles

### 1. Be Human, Not Robotic
❌ "Your request has been processed. Reference ID: 12345."
✅ "Got it! I've created a ticket for you (ID: 12345) and our team will follow up soon."

### 2. Show Empathy
❌ "That feature is not available."
✅ "I understand that would be really helpful. While we don't have that feature yet, here's what you can do instead..."

### 3. Be Concise but Complete
❌ "Click the button."
✅ "Click the blue 'New Project' button in the top right corner of your dashboard."

### 4. Use Positive Language
❌ "You can't do that on the Starter plan."
✅ "That feature is available on our Professional plan. Would you like to learn more about upgrading?"

### 5. Own Mistakes
❌ "The system is down."
✅ "We're experiencing technical issues and our team is working on a fix. I apologize for the inconvenience."

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Email (Formal but Friendly)

**Structure:**
1. Greeting (use name if available)
2. Acknowledge the issue
3. Provide solution
4. Offer additional help
5. Professional sign-off

**Example:**
```
Hi Sarah,

Thanks for reaching out about the Slack integration!

Here's how to connect ProjectFlow with Slack:
1. Go to Settings → Integrations
2. Click "Connect Slack"
3. Authorize ProjectFlow in your Slack workspace
4. Choose which notifications you'd like to receive

The whole process takes about 2 minutes. Once connected, you'll see project updates right in your Slack channels!

Need help with any of these steps? Just reply to this email.

Best regards,
TechCorp Support Team

---
Ticket Reference: [ID]
This response was generated by our AI assistant.
```

**Tone:** Professional, detailed, structured

**Length:** 200-500 words (can be longer for complex issues)

**Formatting:** 
- Use bullet points and numbered lists
- Bold important terms
- Include links to relevant documentation
- Add ticket reference at bottom

### WhatsApp (Conversational & Concise)

**Structure:**
1. Quick acknowledgment
2. Direct answer
3. One follow-up question (if needed)

**Example:**
```
Hey! 👋

To reset your password:
1. Go to login page
2. Click "Forgot Password"
3. Check your email

Should take just a minute! Let me know if you don't get the email. 📧
```

**Tone:** Friendly, casual, quick

**Length:** 50-300 characters (keep it SHORT!)

**Formatting:**
- Use emojis sparingly (1-2 per message)
- Short sentences
- Line breaks for readability
- No formal greetings/sign-offs

**Emojis to Use:**
- 👋 (greeting)
- ✅ (confirmation)
- 📧 (email)
- 📱 (mobile)
- 🔧 (technical)
- 💡 (tip)
- ⚠️ (warning)

**Avoid:** 😂 🤣 ❤️ (too casual)

### Web Form (Semi-Formal, Helpful)

**Structure:**
1. Thank them for contacting
2. Provide clear answer
3. Link to documentation
4. Offer next steps

**Example:**
```
Thanks for reaching out!

To export your project data:
1. Open the project
2. Click the "..." menu in the top right
3. Select "Export"
4. Choose your format (CSV, JSON, or PDF)
5. Click "Download"

You can find more details in our export guide: [link]

Need help with anything else? Feel free to submit another request or check out our knowledge base.
```

**Tone:** Helpful, clear, action-oriented

**Length:** 150-300 words

**Formatting:**
- Numbered steps for instructions
- Include relevant links
- Clear call-to-action

## Language Guidelines

### Words to Use
- "Help" instead of "assist"
- "Fix" instead of "resolve"
- "Issue" instead of "problem"
- "Let me" instead of "I will"
- "You can" instead of "It is possible to"

### Words to Avoid
- "Obviously" (condescending)
- "Simply" or "Just" (minimizes difficulty)
- "Unfortunately" (too negative)
- "As per" (too formal)
- "Kindly" (outdated)
- "Dear Sir/Madam" (too formal)

### Technical Terms
- Explain acronyms on first use
- Link to documentation for complex features
- Use customer's terminology when possible

## Handling Different Situations

### When You Don't Know the Answer
❌ "I don't have that information."
✅ "Great question! Let me connect you with someone who can give you the most accurate answer."

### When There's a Bug
❌ "There's a bug in the system."
✅ "Thanks for reporting this! I've logged it with our engineering team. We'll keep you updated on the fix."

### When Customer is Frustrated
❌ "Please calm down."
✅ "I completely understand your frustration. Let me help you resolve this right away."

### When Escalating
❌ "I can't help you with that."
✅ "I want to make sure you get the best help possible, so I'm connecting you with our [TEAM] team who specializes in this."

### When Customer Says Thank You
❌ "You're welcome."
✅ "Happy to help! Feel free to reach out anytime. 😊"

## Response Time Expectations

### Set Clear Expectations
- Email: "You'll hear back within 24 hours"
- WhatsApp: "Usually respond in a few minutes"
- Web Form: "We typically respond within 5 minutes"

### If Delayed
"Thanks for your patience! This is taking a bit longer than expected. I'm still working on it and will update you within [TIMEFRAME]."

## Accessibility

- Use clear, simple language
- Avoid jargon when possible
- Provide alternative text descriptions for images
- Use proper heading structure in long emails
- Ensure links have descriptive text

## Cultural Sensitivity

- Avoid idioms that don't translate well
- Be mindful of time zones
- Don't assume holidays or working hours
- Use inclusive language
- Respect different communication styles

## Quality Checklist

Before sending any response, verify:
- ✅ Answered the customer's question
- ✅ Tone matches the channel
- ✅ No spelling or grammar errors
- ✅ Links work and are relevant
- ✅ Next steps are clear
- ✅ Empathy is shown (if appropriate)
- ✅ Length is appropriate for channel
